# Rest Day Options: Cocking and Amberley

Research for potential rest days during South Downs Way walk, late April 2026.

---

## 1. Cocking (Mile ~37, Mon 27 Apr)

Cocking is a small village in the heart of the South Downs National Park, sitting in the valley below Cocking Down. It has a pub, a tea room, and a church, but is fundamentally a quiet village. A rest day here would be heavily walking-focused.

### Local Attractions

**St Catherine's Church**
- Norman church with wall paintings dated to around 1220
- Free to visit, worth a look but not a long stop

**Andy Goldsworthy Chalk Stones Trail**
- 14 chalk stone sculptures (each 2-3m across, heaviest 14 tonnes) installed in 2002 along a ~5 mile stretch from Cocking Hill car park to West Dean College
- Carved from chalk quarried at Duncton Quarry, designed to slowly erode back into the landscape
- Free, outdoor, self-guided -- a genuinely interesting art/landscape experience
- **Note:** The stones were only expected to last ~5 years and were installed in 2002, so they may be significantly eroded or gone by 2026. Worth checking before planning a day around this.

**Weald & Downland Living Museum, Singleton** (~2.5 miles from Cocking)
- 40-acre open-air museum with 50+ historic buildings dating from 950 AD to 19th century
- Farm animals, gardens, working mill, craft demonstrations
- Open daily Mar-Oct, 10am-5pm (last entry 3pm)
- Adult: ~£18.75 (book online for discount)
- Could easily fill a half day or more
- Reachable on foot via the Cocking-Singleton circular walk (see below)

**Hollycombe Steam Museum** (~8 miles north, near Liphook)
- Steam railways, traditional fairground, woodland gardens
- Opens Easter weekend; open Sundays and Bank Holidays Apr-Oct, 11:30am-5pm
- Adult £16, Senior £14
- **Too far to walk** -- would need a taxi or lift. Mon 27 Apr is not a bank holiday, so likely **closed**. Not practical.

### Circular Walks from Cocking

**Cocking & Heyshott Circular (short)**
- **Distance:** 3.6 miles (5.8 km)
- **Time:** ~1.5-2 hours
- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Highlights:** Fields, woodland (Hoe Copse with **bluebells in late April**), Marsh Pond, Heyshott village
- **Perfect for a gentle rest-day leg stretch**

**Cocking to Singleton Loop**
- **Distance:** 8.3 miles
- **Ascent:** 520m
- **Time:** ~4-5 hours
- **Highlights:** South Downs Way ridge, Singleton Forest, Levin Down nature reserve (chalk grassland, butterflies), Charlton village, Singleton village
- **Can be combined with a visit to the Weald & Downland Museum in Singleton** -- walk one way via the loop and visit the museum, or do the full circuit
- This is a proper day walk, not really a "rest day" option given the ascent

**Cocking & West Dean Woods**
- **Distance:** 7.5 miles
- **Highlights:** Climb to SDW ridge, circuit through West Dean Woods
- **Includes the Chalk Stones Trail** if you route via Cocking Hill

**Short Cocking Circular**
- **Distance:** 2 miles
- **Time:** ~1 hour
- **A very easy stroll**

### Food & Drink

**The Blue Bell**
- Community-owned village pub, quality home-cooked food, Sunday roasts
- Opening hours: Closed Monday, Tue 17:30-21:30, Wed-Sat 11:00-21:30, Sun 12:00-17:00
- **CLOSED MONDAYS** -- if the rest day is Mon 27 Apr, the pub will be shut. This is a significant drawback.
- Phone: 01730 239669

**Moonlight Cottage Tea Rooms**
- In the village, for daytime refreshment
- Opening hours not confirmed -- may also have limited days

### Verdict: Cocking as a Rest Day

**Marginal.** Cocking is a quiet village with limited amenities. The main draw would be the Heyshott bluebell walk (lovely in late April) and potentially combining a longer walk to Singleton with the Weald & Downland Museum. However:

- **The Blue Bell pub is closed on Mondays** -- if staying Mon night, you'd arrive to a shut pub and limited evening food options (Manor Farm B&B includes breakfast but not dinner)
- The village has no shop
- It would suit someone happy with a quiet day of walking and countryside, but could feel quite limited for a full rest day
- **Best used as:** A half-day activity (morning bluebell walk + tea room) rather than a full rest day

## 2. Amberley (Mile ~48.5, Tue 28 Apr)

Amberley is a picture-postcard village with thatched cottages, a castle, two pubs, a tea room, a pottery, and -- crucially -- a proper visitor attraction (Amberley Museum) and excellent walking in all directions. It also has a railway station for easy access to Arundel or further afield.

### Local Attractions

**Amberley Museum & Heritage Centre**
- Industrial and transport heritage museum in a former chalk quarry
- Narrow gauge railway rides, working print shop, lime kilns, road steam engines, vintage bus service
- The quarry was used as a filming location for the James Bond film *A View to a Kill*
- Open Wed-Sun + Bank Holidays, 10am-4:30pm (mid-Feb to Nov)
- **Open Tue 28 Apr? Normally closed Tuesdays.** But if taking a rest day Wed 29, it would be open. Check calendar.
- Adult: £14.50, Senior (60+): £12.50
- Allow 3-4 hours for a thorough visit
- 2-for-1 with National Rail tickets

**Amberley Castle**
- 900-year-old castle, now a luxury hotel
- Not open as a visitor attraction, but you can book afternoon tea (~£35-45pp) or peer through the gateway
- The exterior and grounds are atmospheric

**Amberley Wildbrooks (RSPB/Sussex Wildlife Trust)**
- 800 acres of flood meadows in the Arun Valley, north of the village
- One of the richest remaining wetlands in the UK
- **Late April is excellent for birdwatching:** skylarks, meadow pipits, reed buntings, warblers, lapwing, redshank displaying, cuckoo calling
- Walk from the village through the Wildbrooks along the Wey South Path
- Free, open access on footpaths (can be boggy -- boots essential)
- Allow 2-3 hours for a gentle explore

**Bignor Roman Villa** (~3 miles north via footpaths)
- Extraordinarily well-preserved Roman mosaics (some of the finest in Britain)
- Open Apr-Oct, Wed-Sun, 10am-4:30pm
- Tearoom, gift shop, picnic area
- Reachable on foot via the SDW to Bignor Hill then down (a proper walk -- see below)
- Phone: 01798 869259

**Parham House & Gardens** (~3.5 miles east)
- Elizabethan house with important art collection, needlework, paintings
- Third longest Long Gallery in England
- 4-acre walled garden with herbaceous borders
- Open Wed, Thu, Fri, Sun + Bank Holidays from Easter (5 Apr 2026)
- Gardens 12pm-5pm, House 2pm-5pm
- Adult: £16, Gardens only: £12
- Reachable on foot but a full day if walking both ways

**Amberley Village Pottery**
- Working pottery on Church Street by Caroline Seaton
- Showroom open Thu-Sun, 11am-3pm
- Free to browse

### Circular Walks from Amberley

**River Arun Circular (short)**
- **Distance:** ~3.3 miles (5.3 km)
- **Time:** ~1.5 hours
- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Highlights:** River Arun, Houghton Bridge, views of Amberley Castle, thatched cottages, Amberley Village Tea Room en route
- **Ideal gentle rest-day walk**

**Amberley & Bignor Hill Circular**
- **Distance:** ~9.5 miles (15.3 km)
- **Ascent:** 436m
- **Time:** 4.5-5 hours
- **Highlights:** SDW ridge with panoramic views, neolithic camp on Bignor Hill, descent to Bignor Roman Villa (with mosaics visit), return via Monarch's Way
- **A proper day walk** -- combines great ridge walking with a genuine historic attraction
- Could shorten by visiting the villa and returning the same way (~6 miles)

**Amberley to Arundel Circular**
- **Distance:** ~12 miles
- **Time:** ~6 hours
- **Highlights:** SDW ridge, descent to Arundel (castle, cathedral, town, pubs, shops), riverside return
- **Too long for a rest day** but Arundel is also reachable by train (1 stop from Amberley station, 5 min)

**Amberley Wildbrooks Loop**
- **Distance:** ~4-5 miles
- **Time:** 2-3 hours
- **Highlights:** Wetland meadows, birdlife, river views, peaceful flat walking
- Can be muddy; stick to Wey South Path

### Food & Drink

**The George and Dragon**
- One of the oldest pubs in Sussex
- Food and drinks, atmospheric

**The Sportsman**
- The other village pub (also potential accommodation at £120/night)
- Good food

**Amberley Village Tea Room**
- Highly rated (4.7/5 on TripAdvisor), homemade cakes, served on pottery crockery
- **Note:** Website says "Coming Soon" which may indicate changes -- worth checking closer to the date

**Riverside Tea Rooms** (near Houghton Bridge, ~1 mile)
- Fully licensed restaurant and tea rooms
- Alfresco terrace dining with river views
- Locally sourced food, cream teas

### Getting to Arundel

Amberley station to Arundel station is one stop on the Arun Valley line (Southern Railway). Trains roughly every 30 min, journey time ~5 min. Arundel offers:
- Arundel Castle (major attraction, allow 3+ hours, ~£22 adult)
- Arundel Cathedral
- Independent shops, cafes, restaurants
- Arundel Wetland Centre (WWT)
- A proper small town with plenty to fill a day

### Verdict: Amberley as a Rest Day

**Excellent.** Amberley has enough variety to fill a full rest day comfortably, with multiple options depending on energy levels:

- **Low energy:** Village wander, pottery, tea room, pub lunch, Wildbrooks stroll (2-3 hours walking)
- **Medium energy:** River Arun circular (1.5 hrs) + Amberley Museum (3-4 hrs) = full day
- **Higher energy:** Bignor Hill circular with Roman Villa visit (5+ hrs)
- **Non-walking option:** Train to Arundel for castle, cathedral, shops, lunch

The village itself is genuinely beautiful and worth time just pottering around. Two pubs, a tea room, a pottery, and excellent birdwatching on the doorstep. The museum alone could fill half a day.

**Key caveat:** Amberley Museum is normally closed Tuesdays. If the rest day falls on Tue 28 Apr, the museum won't be open. However, it should be open on a Wednesday. Check their specific 2026 calendar.

## Comparison & Recommendation

| Factor | Cocking | Amberley |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Village charm | Pleasant but quiet | Genuinely beautiful, thatched cottages |
| Pubs | 1 (closed Mondays) | 2 (both serving food) |
| Tea rooms | 1 (limited hours?) | 2 (village + Riverside) |
| Shops | None | Pottery + small village shop |
| Major attraction | Weald & Downland Museum (2.5mi away) | Amberley Museum (in village) |
| Walking options | Good (bluebells in April) | Excellent (variety of distances) |
| Nature/wildlife | Chalk downland, woodland | Wildbrooks wetland (RSPB), river |
| Historic sites | Church (1220 paintings) | Bignor Roman Villa (walkable), Arundel Castle (by train) |
| Transport links | None (bus only, infrequent) | Railway station (Arundel 5 min, London ~1.5 hrs) |
| Can fill a whole day? | Barely | Easily |
| Best for rest day? | Half-day at most | Full day, multiple options |

**Recommendation: Amberley is by far the better rest day location.** It has the variety, the attractions, and the infrastructure to support a genuinely enjoyable day off the trail. Cocking works better as an overnight stop on a walking day rather than a rest day destination.

If you want a rest day, plan it for Amberley. A suggested itinerary:
- Morning: Gentle walk through Amberley Wildbrooks for birdwatching (2 hrs)
- Lunch: George and Dragon or Riverside Tea Rooms
- Afternoon: Amberley Museum (if open) or village potter + pottery visit
- Or: Train to Arundel for the afternoon
