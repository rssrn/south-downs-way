**Status:** Web-researched March 2026. Prices are indicative — phone/check availability for 29 April 2026.

## Trail Geography

The SDW runs along the ridge from Amberley, through Washington (mile 55, A24 crossing), then continues east along the ridge past Steyning Bowl. The trail does NOT go through Steyning itself — Steyning is about 1 mile off-trail to the north, down in a valley at ~20m elevation.

The trail crosses the River Adur at Upper Beeding (mile 62), then climbs to Truleigh Hill (mile 64, ~210m). Upper Beeding is ON the trail at the river crossing.

Key locations relative to the trail:
- **Steyning** — 1mi off trail (at mile 58.5), ~20m elevation. Descent from ridge ~150m.
- **Bramber** — adjacent to Steyning, ~0.8mi off trail, ~15m elevation.
- **Upper Beeding** — ON trail at River Adur crossing (mile 62), ~10m elevation.
- **Botolphs** — tiny hamlet between Bramber and Upper Beeding, near trail.
- **Truleigh Hill** — ON trail (mile 64), ~210m elevation. YHA hostel here.

**Important:** Staying in Steyning/Bramber means descending ~150m off the ridge, then re-ascending the next morning. Upper Beeding is better positioned as it's ON the trail at the river crossing — you'd walk through it anyway heading east.

- **Rooms:** 3 rooms — double, twin/single, family. All en-suite shower.

- **Breakfast:** Full English with local produce, homemade granola, OJ, tea/coffee. Served 7:30–9:30am (Mon–Sat), 8:00–10:00am (Sun).

### Staging considerations:

If staying in **Steyning/Bramber (mile ~59):**
- Day 5 (Amberley → Steyning): ~10 miles + 1mi off-trail = 11mi. Moderate.
- Day 6 (Steyning → Pyecombe): 1mi back to trail + 9.5mi = 10.5mi. Moderate.
- Penalty: 2mi extra walking + ~150m climb back to ridge.

If staying in **Upper Beeding (mile 62):**
- Day 5 (Amberley → Upper Beeding): ~13.5 miles. Longer but all on-trail.
- Day 6 (Upper Beeding → Pyecombe): ~6 miles. Short/easy day.
- Benefit: No detour, no climbing penalty. But Day 5 is longer.

If staying at **YHA Truleigh Hill (mile 64):**
- Day 5 (Amberley → Truleigh Hill): ~15.5 miles. Long day with significant climbing.
- Day 6 (Truleigh Hill → Pyecombe): ~4 miles. Very short.
- Probably too long for Day 5 given the couple's pace.
