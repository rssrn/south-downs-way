# Accommodation Research: Buriton Area (SDW Mile ~23-29)

Research date: 2026-03-13. Prices are best estimates from web research; confirm directly before booking.

## Summary

The Buriton area has limited but adequate accommodation. The cheapest proper indoor option is the South Downs Eco Lodge hostel (from £35pp in a twin). Buriton village itself has two options but neither is budget: The Nest Hotel (~£130+/night) and The Five Bells self-catering cottages (price unknown but likely £80-100+). Copper Beeches B&B between Petersfield and South Harting is the best-value B&B found (~£60 single occ). Premier Inn Petersfield is worth considering as a reliable budget chain option but is 2.5mi off trail.

- **Address:** High Street, Buriton, Petersfield, Hampshire
- **Distance from SDW:** ~0.5 miles (in Buriton village)
- **Price:** From ~£130/night (double room) based on Booking.com/KAYAK data. This is NOT a budget option.

## PETERSFIELD (Mile ~25.5, 2.5mi off trail) - Budget options only

### Premier Inn Petersfield
- **Type:** Budget chain hotel
- **Address:** Near town centre/station, Petersfield
- **Distance from SDW:** ~2.5 miles
- **Price:** Typically £50-80/night for a double room (varies by date; check website)
- **Breakfast:** Extra (~£10-13pp for full breakfast)
- **Contact:** Via premierinn.com
- **Website:** premierinn.com/gb/en/hotels/england/hampshire/petersfield/petersfield.html
- **Notes:** 81 rooms. Reliable chain quality. 10-minute walk from Petersfield station. Bus links available. No personality but guaranteed standards. Getting back to the trail from Petersfield adds 2.5mi + significant climb back up to the ridge. Petersfield has a taxi rank if needed.

### Other Petersfield options noted but not fully researched:

- **Pound Orchard B&B** - adults only, garden, terrace, free parking. Prices unknown.

## BETWEEN BURITON AND SOUTH HARTING (on or near trail)

### Copper Beeches B&B
- **Type:** Farmhouse B&B + self-catering cottages
- **Address:** Torberry Farm, Hurst, Nr. Petersfield, Hampshire, GU31 5RG
- **Distance from SDW:** Short walk - public footpath opposite leads to SDW. Approximately 15 minutes walk. Located between Petersfield and South Harting.
- **Price:**
  - Single occupancy: ~£60/night
  - Double occupancy: ~£90-115/night (variable by season/platform)
  - Self-catering cottages: £350-450/week (£50-65/night equivalent)
- **Breakfast:** INCLUDED for B&B rooms. Excellent home-produced breakfast: own pork sausages, bacon, free-range eggs, homemade bread and jams.
- **Rooms:** 2 en-suite B&B rooms (1 double, 1 family suite). 2 self-catering cottages (1 bedroom each, sofa bed available).
- **Contact:** 01730 826662 / 07976 234030 / info@copperbeeches.net
- **Website:** copperbeeches.net
- **Notes:** TripAdvisor reviews specifically recommend for SDW walkers. Dog-friendly. Free parking, WiFi, cycle storage. Rural location in South Downs National Park. ONLY 2 B&B ROOMS - book well in advance.

## RECOMMENDATIONS (price-sensitive walkers)

### Best value options, ranked by price:

1. **South Downs Eco Lodge - Hostel twin** (~£35pp/night, self-catering) - Cheapest option. ON the trail. But it's at mile ~18-19, about 5 miles before QECP, so you'd need to factor this into stage planning. Would make Day 2 from Exton shorter (~7mi) and Day 3 longer.

2. **Copper Beeches B&B** (~£60 single / ~£90-115 double, breakfast included) - Best value B&B. 15 min walk from trail. Excellent home-cooked breakfast. Only 2 rooms so book early.

3. **Premier Inn Petersfield** (~£50-80/night, breakfast extra) - Reliable and often cheap if booked early. But 2.5mi off trail and adds a significant climb back up to the ridge next morning.

4. **The Five Bells, Buriton** (price unknown - contact directly) - Self-catering cottages in village 0.5mi from trail. Could be good value for a couple if priced reasonably. Popular with walkers.

5. **The White Hart, South Harting** (from ~£95+/night) - Nice pub with rooms but 1mi off trail and not cheap. Consider only if other options full. Note: South Harting is 3 miles further along the trail from Buriton, which would mean a longer Day 2 or shorter Day 3.

6. **South Downs Eco Lodge - B&B** (from ~£70/night, breakfast included) - Good value for en-suite B&B on the trail, but same location caveat as the hostel.

### Key considerations:
- Buriton is in a valley below the ridge. Staying there means descending ~100m in the evening and re-ascending next morning.
- The Sustainability Centre/Eco Lodge is ON the ridge but is ~5mi before QECP, not near Buriton.
- Copper Beeches is the sweet spot: near the trail, between Buriton and South Harting, good breakfast, reasonable price.
- There is NO accommodation actually at Queen Elizabeth Country Park itself.
