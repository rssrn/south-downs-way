## Staging Discussion

### The accommodation landscape problem
The SDW is a ridge walk but most accommodation is in valleys below. Every night in a valley costs ~100–200m extra climbing (down in the evening, back up next morning). The exceptions are:
- Chilcomb (mile 2.5) — on trail
- Crossways, Exton (mile 12.5) — valley but on trail
- SD Eco Lodge (mile ~17) — on trail, ON the ridge
- Hampshire Hog (mile ~25.5 area) — at 140m, same as trail low point

### The 25-mile accommodation desert
Between the Eco Lodge (mile ~17) and Cocking (mile ~37) there are only thin options around Buriton (mile 25.5). Butser Hill (the highest and hardest climb) sits right in this gap. This makes Night 2 staging critical.

### Alternative staging: "The Chilcomb trick"
If the Friday arrival doesn't work out, the Chilcomb trick remains viable for a Saturday arrival:
- Sat afternoon: Walk 2.5mi Winchester → Chilcomb (Complyns £70)
- Sun Day 1: Chilcomb → Eco Lodge (14.5mi, ~350–400m ascent) — £70 hostel
- Mon Day 2: Eco Lodge → Buriton/Clanfield (8.5mi over Butser Hill) — £107

This covers the same ground for £219 over 3 nights but Day 1 is 14.5mi (at the upper limit of comfort). The Friday arrival plan spreads the miles more evenly.
